What defines an insect?




Insects can fly!


Wings have helped insects move around. So when food becomes hard to find in one area, they can fly to another place with food.

Their Anatomy


Insects have three pairs of jointed legs, segmented bodies, an exoskeleton, one pair of antennae, and usually one or two pairs of wings.

They can adapt


There are many ways that insects have changed their behavior in some way to live or feed on different plants or other insects.

Their Diet


Insects will eat many things like plants in our gardens or wood and some feed on one thing or many.

Here are a couple of different types insects below!

Ants form colonies that range in size from a few dozen to millions of ants. The colonies are described as superorganisms because the ants appear to operate as a unified entity, collectively working together to support the colony. Find out more here.

Bees are winged insects closely related to wasps and ants, known for their roles in pollination and, in the case of the best-known bee species, the western honey bee, for producing honey. Some species including honey bees, bumblebees, and stingless bees live socially in colonies while most other species are solitary. Find out more here.

Beetles have their front pair of wings hardened into wing-cases, distinguishing them from most other insects. They are found in almost every habitat except the sea and the polar regions.They often feed on plants and fungi, break down animal and plant debris, and eat other invertebrates. Some species are serious pests while others are helpful by eating aphids and other plant-eating insects. Find out more here.

Mantises are found worldwide in temperate and tropical habitats. They have triangular heads with bulging eyes supported on flexible necks. Their elongated bodies may or may not have wings, but all mantises have forelegs that are enlarged and adapted for catching and gripping prey. Their upright posture, while remaining stationary has led to the common name praying mantis. Find out more here.
